Folder 1. Euphorbia exigua [G]. i. "I found this plant, 1773, in our Cornfields, and supposed it to be only a variety of the exigua. I am still of the same opinion. It agrees indeed pretty well with the Tishy-malus Segetum longifolius. Cat. Cant. App & Rall Syn. p.312, since it is there described as Esulae exiguae Tragi persimilis, sed major; wch last circumstance may easily be accounted for; *because of the Umbella quinquefida in the one & trifida in the other. The Segetalis in Ray and the Segetalis in Linnaeus are certainly two very different Plants; and I should be glad to know, and I wish it may be in your power to inform me, whether those Specimens, wch Mr. Lyons found near Triplow, be at all different from this Specimen—another circumstance also makes me conclude that it is only a variety of the exigua; because found in company wth it, and am inclined to think that it never will be found anywhere else. I gathered more of it at the same Time; but have either mislaid or shuffled it away. But if I should find the same variety again, I will endeavour to collect the Seed of it, and try what it will do in my Garden. *Supposing all other circumstances equal, the quinquefida must be to the trifida in Magnitude in the Proportion of 5 to 3, wch wants only one third part of being twice as big" [B]. ii.
